Wikibuy Mobile App Redesign
The Problem
The existing Wikibuy mobile application had issues with usability, stability, new user attrition, and a lack of overall revenue generation for the business. My design team and I were tasked with shoring up the customer experience, improving overall engagement, and increasing downstream effects to potential revenue.
My Role
  • Design Management
  • Research
  • Wireframing
  • UX/UI Design
  • Prototyping
  • Design System Development
I worked closely with our product and research team to identify the largest opportunities for improving the overall customer experience and increase mobile purchases through our app. I guided my design team into creating a series of viable solutions for improved user flows and net new functionality that could be implemented on shortened delivery timelines with our engineering team.
The Solution
After conducting a series of qualitative interviews with our customers, analyzing app usage, and examining performance data we identified the largest pain points and opportunities to improve the experience. We then redesigned a series of core flows within the app including a completely new personalized home page, improved search experience, new onboarding flow with push notification enrollment, redesigned price comparison experience. We also designed and built a new in-app shopping flow that allowed us to integrate our flagship Automatic Coupon service. This gave the app full feature parity with our core desktop browser extension product and brought 30% more savings to customers shopping through the app.
Results
  • The new homepage design led to a 25% increase in purchases year-over-year.
  • The redesigned price comparison experience increased purchases by around 40% year-over-year.
  • In the process we also rebranded from Wikibuy to the new Capital One Shopping brand in time for a national advertising launch in Fall 2020.
  • App store rating increases from 3.3 to 4.7 stars
