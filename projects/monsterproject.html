The Monster Project

Challenge

Inspire kids.

Solution

For the past few years I’ve organized this incredibly rewarding project for my wife’s 1st grade students. It’s called The Monster Project.

Each year, the responses we get from the children are not only amazing, but incredibly moving for me and everyone involved. These range from uncontrollable happiness to shrieking excitement. These reactions are sparked by presenting artistic interpretations like this:

I started doing this project after my wife read about the amazing group of creatives behind the Monster Project. The project was created with the simple mission of showing students the power of their own creativity. The way it works is first, students create a monster using their imagination. It is then given to a professional artist who reimagines it in their own style. The work is presented back to the students and that is where the real magic happens.

I’ve worked as a professional designer for over a decade and have gotten to know many many talented creatives. Realizing this, my wife asked if I would rally them together and start doing this project for students at her elementary school. I’m very happy to say that over 500 students later, the impact we’ve had on the kids and artists involved is better than I ever could have imagined.

Year after year, around 100 artists across my network volunteer their time to be a part of this project. Huge shout out to everyone I’ve had the pleasure of collaborating with because without each and every one of them, this would not be possible.
